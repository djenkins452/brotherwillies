{# ── 1. Getting Started ─────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

1. Getting Started

Brother Willies is a sports analytics platform that compares a mathematical house model against the betting market to identify where they disagree. The site covers College Basketball (CBB), College Football (CFB), MLB, College Baseball, and Golf.

Important: This site is for informational and entertainment purposes only. It does not provide betting advice, picks, or guarantees. All analysis is model-based and reflects statistical probabilities, not certainties.

How it works: The house model calculates a win probability for each game based on team ratings, home-field advantage, and injury data. This probability is compared against the market's implied probability (derived from betting odds). The difference is called the edge — a positive edge means the model thinks the home team is more likely to win than the market does.

Data sources: Schedules and scores come from ESPN and sport-specific APIs (CFBD for CFB, CBBD for CBB, the official statsapi.mlb.com for MLB — including probable pitchers — and ESPN's D1 college-baseball scoreboard). Betting odds come from The Odds API. Injury reports come from ESPN. All data refreshes automatically.

{# ── 2. Dashboard ────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

2. Dashboard

The home page shows a snapshot of what's happening right now.

Live Games: When games are in progress, they appear at the top with a pulsing LIVE badge and current scores. Live data updates each time the page refreshes.

Top Value Games: Below live games, you'll see the upcoming games with the highest house edge for each sport currently in season. These are sorted by the size of the disagreement between the model and the market.

What the numbers mean on each game card:

  • Mkt — Market implied probability (from betting odds)
  • House — House model edge vs market (green = model favors home more than market, red = less)
{# ── 3. Lobby ──────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

3. Lobby

The Lobby is the main analytics page. It shows all upcoming games sorted by edge size.

Sport Tabs: Tabs at the top let you switch between CBB, CFB, and Golf. Only sports with upcoming games or events appear. The default tab follows the current season (CBB in winter, CFB in fall).

Game Sections: Games are grouped into collapsible sections: Today, Tomorrow, This Week, and Coming Up. CFB also shows a "Big Matchups" section for high-rated games. Click section headers to expand or collapse them — your preference is saved.

Sorting: Use the filter chips to sort by:

  • House Edge — Difference between house model and market (default)
  • Your Edge — Difference between your customized model and market
  • Delta — Difference between your model and the house model

Game Cards: Each card shows a compact data grid with Market, House, and Your probabilities, plus edge values. Your favorite team's card gets a colored accent bar at the top.

Confidence Badges: Each game has a confidence indicator: HIGH (odds are fresh and injury data exists), MED (odds within 12 hours), LOW (stale odds or missing data).

Filters: Set minimum edge, spread range, and odds range in Preferences to filter out games that don't meet your criteria.

{# ── 4. Game Detail ──────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

4. Game Detail

Click any game card to see the full analysis for that matchup.

Status Badges: SCHEDULED game hasn't started, LIVE game is in progress with current scores, FINAL game is complete with final scores.

Probability Table: The main table shows three columns:

  • Market — Implied probability from current betting odds
  • House — The site's model probability and edge vs market
  • You — Your customized model probability and edge (if you've tuned weights in My Model)

AI Insight: At the top of the page, an AI-generated analysis explains why the model and market agree or disagree. This uses the game data plus general sports knowledge to provide context. The AI tone is controlled by your persona setting in Preferences.

Injuries: Known injuries are listed with impact levels: HIGH (key player out), MED (rotation player), LOW (minor role). The model adjusts its probability based on these.

Line Movement: If the odds have shifted, you'll see an arrow indicator: ▲ up (odds moved toward home team) or ▼ down (moved away).

{# ── 5. My Model ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

5. My Model

My Model lets you customize the house model by adjusting weight multipliers. The house model uses a default weight of 1.0 for each factor. Increasing a weight amplifies that factor's influence; decreasing it reduces it.

Weight Sliders:

  • Rating Weight — How much team strength ratings matter. Higher = more weight on the better-rated team.
  • Home Field Advantage — How much playing at home matters. Set to 0 if you think home field doesn't matter.
  • Injury Weight — How much injury reports affect the prediction. Higher = injuries matter more.
  • Recent Form — How much recent performance trends matter.
  • Conference Weight — How much conference strength matters.

Presets: Save your weight configurations as named presets in Presets. Load a preset to instantly apply a saved configuration. Reset to defaults restores all weights to 1.0.

{# ── 6. Preferences ──────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

6. Preferences

Preferences controls how the site is personalized for you.

AI Persona: Choose the tone for AI Insights on game detail pages:

  • Analyst — Neutral, professional, data-driven (default)
  • New York Bookie — Blunt, street-smart, no-nonsense
  • Southern Commentator — Folksy, warm, confident
  • Ex-Player — Direct, experiential, locker-room perspective

Favorites: Set your favorite CFB conference and team, CBB team, and golf player. Favorite teams are highlighted on the Lobby with a colored accent bar and "Your Team" badge. Check "Always include favorite team" to ensure your team appears on the Lobby even if it doesn't meet your filter criteria.

Lobby Filters: Set minimum edge, spread range (min/max), and odds range to filter the Lobby to only show games that match your criteria.

Location: Enter your zip code to display game times in your local timezone.

{# ── 7. My Stats ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

7. My Statistics

My Stats tracks your activity on the site. Use the time period filters (7 days, 30 days, Season) to adjust the window.

  • Games Analyzed — Number of games you've viewed or evaluated
  • Favorite Team Games — How many of those involved your favorite team
  • Average House Edge — The average edge on games you looked at
  • Average User Edge — Same, but using your custom model weights
  • Agreement Rate — How often your model and the house model agree on which team wins
  • Confidence Distribution — Breakdown of HIGH/MED/LOW confidence games you viewed

Past performance does not predict future outcomes.

{# ── 8. Model Performance ────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

8. Model Performance

Model Performance shows how well the house model has predicted outcomes for completed games. Metrics are computed from snapshots captured before each game.

Key Metrics:

  • Accuracy — Percentage of games where the model correctly predicted the winner (probability > 50% matched the actual outcome)
  • Brier Score — Calibration quality. 0.000 = perfect predictions. 0.250 = coin flip. Lower is better.
  • By Sport — Separate accuracy and Brier score for CFB and CBB
  • Trends — Performance over the last 7 days and 30 days

Closing Line Value (CLV): Measures whether the market moved toward the model's prediction by game time. Positive CLV means the model identified value before the market caught up. This is considered one of the strongest indicators of model quality.

Calibration Table: Groups predictions by probability range (50-60%, 60-70%, etc.) and compares the predicted win rate against the actual win rate. A well-calibrated model has actual rates close to the predicted rates in each bucket.

How snapshots work: The system captures the house model's prediction and the market probability for each game within 24 hours of start time. After the game completes, the actual outcome and closing odds are recorded. Performance metrics are computed from these snapshots.

Past performance does not predict future outcomes.

{# ── 9. Parlays ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

9. Parlays

The Parlay Builder is an analytics tool for evaluating multi-game parlays. It does not place bets — it computes combined probabilities using the house model.

Building a Parlay: Select 2 or more games, choose a side for each (home or away moneyline), and click Analyze. The system calculates each leg's probability and multiplies them together.

Correlation Risk: If your parlay includes games from the same conference or division, the system flags potential correlation. Correlated outcomes mean the legs aren't truly independent, which affects the combined probability.

10% Haircut: The combined probability is reduced by 10% to account for the inherent difficulty of hitting multi-leg parlays and the vig (sportsbook margin) built into parlay payouts.

This tool is for analytical comparison only. It does not recommend or encourage parlay betting.

{# ── Baseball (MLB + College Baseball) ──────────────────────────────── #}

Baseball (MLB + College Baseball)

Baseball is a first-class sport in Brother Willies — MLB games appear on their own MLB Hub and in the Lobby tab, and college baseball games appear on the College Baseball Hub and Lobby tab.

Starting pitchers are the primary driver. Our baseball house model weights the starting-pitcher-rating gap at 65% and the team-rating gap at 35%, with a modest home-field adjustment (MLB ≈ 2.5, College Baseball ≈ 2.0 on our logistic scale). A pitcher's rating is derived from their season ERA, WHIP, and K/9 and ranges roughly 10 (poor) to 95 (elite).

Missing pitcher data — We never invent a pitcher when the starter hasn't been announced. Games with a "Probable pitcher TBD" marker automatically show lower confidence in the model output. ESPN does not expose probable pitchers for college baseball, so most CB games currently have TBD pitchers; this is surfaced plainly.

Bet types — Moneyline, run line (baseball's equivalent of the point spread — MLB run lines are almost always ±1.5), and total runs over/under. All three settle against real final scores pulled from the live feed.

Data sources — MLB schedule / teams / probable pitchers / live scores come from the official statsapi.mlb.com (no API key required). College baseball schedule and scores come from ESPN's public scoreboard (groups=50, full D1). Odds for both come from The Odds API. If a provider is unreachable, the UI says "Data temporarily unavailable" rather than fabricating content.

MLB tile priority layer — The MLB Hub ranks games before rendering. Live and today's games appear as horizontal tiles with a priority chip (Priority / Watch) and a short "why" line. Priority is computed from tight spread, close live score, key injury impact, and ace pitching matchup; blowout live games and TBD-pitcher games get demoted. Games beyond today stay in a chronological list. All priority logic lives in apps/mlb/services/prioritization.py — templates only render.

Guided choice: Primary vs Secondary — Action tags carry a strength. When both 💰 Best Bet and 🔥 Watch Now fire on the same game, Best Bet becomes the Primary action (filled pill) and Watch Now becomes Secondary (outlined). Exactly one Primary per game. Nothing is hidden — the hierarchy makes the best spot obvious. A gold ★ Top Opportunity tag appears on only one game per page by default: the single highest-confidence Best Bet across live + today. Configurable via settings.MLB_MAX_TOP_OPPORTUNITIES.

Line-value signal — line_value_discrepancy = |house_prob − market_prob|. When the gap is ≥ 6%, the game gets a Model edge vs. market reason and Best Bet attaches that reason in preference to a tight-spread trigger. This is a real "we see something the market doesn't" signal, not just a thin-line bet.

Action tags & one-click mock bets — MLB tiles carry up to two action tags. 🔥 Watch Now marks close live games or ace pitching matchups. 💰 Best Bet marks games where the market spread is tight (≤1.5) and both starters are known — a TBD pitcher always suppresses Best Bet because our model has less information. Logged-in users also see a Mock Bet button on each tile that opens the bet modal with the smart-default selection, bet type, and odds already filled in. The Selection field is a two-option dropdown keyed to the current bet type (both teams for moneyline, both signed spreads for spread, Over/Under for total). Override anything before submitting.

Tile context — Each tile shows both teams' season records, a streak chip when a team is on a run of 2+ W or L games, and each starting pitcher's season W-L record next to their name. If you're logged in and already have a pending mock bet on the game, a 🎯 icon appears in the tile header and a thin green rail lights the right edge.

{# ── 10. Mock Bets ──────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

10. Mock Bets

The Mock Bets system lets you simulate betting decisions and track outcomes over time. No real money is involved — this is a decision-quality analysis tool.

Placing a Mock Bet (single): From any game detail page (CFB, CBB, MLB, or College Baseball), tap "Place Mock Bet" to open the placement modal. Choose a bet type (moneyline, spread, total), enter your selection and odds, set a simulated stake amount, and choose a confidence level and model source (House or Your Model). When the model has produced a recommendation for the game, the button reads "Place Mock Bet on Model Pick" and the modal pre-fills with the recommendation's pick and odds.

Source-Aware Betting — what the colored badges mean: Every odd we display is tagged with where it came from. The badge appears on every MLB tile and on the MLB game-detail page next to the existing "Data: HIGH" pill:

  • 🟢 Verified Odds — the line came from the paid Odds API (authoritative). Recommendations behave normally; this is the trust tier we'd use for a real-money decision.
  • 🟡 ESPN Odds — the line came from ESPN's free fallback feed. Used when primary data is missing or stale. Recommendations still issue, but the displayed confidence is reduced (×0.85) to reflect lower trust in the source.
  • 🔴 Derived Odds — only one side of the line was published, and we synthesized the other via symmetric inversion. Never feeds a recommendation; never appears in bulk-bet buttons. Visible only in staff diagnostics.

Tiered Intelligence Phase 2 — Lean Layer (when enabled): Spread + total opportunity signals can be promoted to Lean status when their historical win rate clears the threshold. A Lean is shown on the card with a yellow badge: 🟡 Lean followed by the supporting stats (e.g., "56.0% win rate, 42 games"). Lean ≠ Recommended — the yellow badge is intentionally distinct from the green Recommended tier so the trust levels never blur.

  • Spread Lean: requires historical win rate > 53% over a 30+ game sample.
  • Total Lean: requires historical win rate > 54% AND ROI > 2% over a 30+ game sample (stricter because total markets are noisier).
  • Pushes excluded from win-rate math (standard sports-betting convention) but counted in raw totals for transparency.
  • Snapshotted at create time — once a signal is stamped Lean (or not), threshold tweaks won't retroactively re-label it. The data on the badge is the data the signal saw when it fired.
  • "🟡 Leans Only" filter chip on the MLB hub isolates just the data-supported leans across spread + total.
  • Bulk betting on Lean opportunities is disabled — Lean expresses evidence, not modeled edge.
  • New "Spread & Total Performance" panel at /profile/performance/ — per-signal-type win rate, ROI estimate, sample, pushes, Lean? marker.

Tiered Intelligence Phase 1 — Spread + Total opportunity signals (when enabled): Alongside the model-backed Moneyline recommendations, the MLB hub can also surface rule-based opportunity signals for the run-line and totals markets. These are NOT recommendations and are never mixed into "Bet All" actions. Each card carries a "Not model-backed — informational only" disclaimer at both the section level and the per-card level. The bet-type filter chip bar at the top of the hub ([All] [Moneyline] [Spread] [Total]) lets you isolate one bet type at a time. Bulk buttons for Spread and Total render as Coming Soon — Phase 2 will activate them once we have a modeled edge for those markets.

  • Spread signals: Tight Spread (|spread| ≤ 1.5) or Large Favorite (|spread| ≥ 2.5). The 1.5–2.5 gap intentionally produces no signal — silence is correct, not a bug.
  • Total signals: High Scoring (total ≥ 9.5) or Low Scoring (total ≤ 7.5). The 7.5–9.5 gap is similarly silent.
  • Source attribution carries through — if the underlying snapshot was 🟡 ESPN, the signal card shows it.

MLB hub decision groups: Today's games are organized into two clearly-bordered group boxes: 👍 Recommended Plays (green) wraps Top Plays + Recommended Bets (verified) + Recommended Bets (ESPN); ⚠️ Not Recommended (gray) wraps today's fades. The Not Recommended box always renders — if there's nothing to fade today, you see a positive empty-state instead of silent absence.

Bulk bet buttons (MLB hub): The MLB hub header has split bulk-bet buttons that match what's visible on screen. Each button shows its count in the label so you see how many bets you'll place before you click:

  • 🟢 Bet All Verified Plays (N) — places a $100 mock bet on every game whose recommendation uses paid Odds API data (the authoritative source). This is the “Top Plays + Recommended Bets” cohort from the green Recommended Plays group box.
  • 🟡 Bet All ESPN Plays (N) — only appears when there are ESPN-fallback recommendations. Places a $100 bet on each, with a stronger confirm modal that explicitly warns you about lower-trust source.
  • The confirm modal tells you exactly how many bets are about to land: “You are about to place a $100 mock bet on each of 8 recommended games …”. Games where you already have a pending bet are skipped automatically and reported in the success toast.
  • Both buttons are scoped to today's local slate (matches the visible Top Plays + Recommended sections). Tomorrow's games are listed under "Upcoming" with no bulk action attached.
  • Transactional and idempotent — running twice will not create duplicates.

Bulk: "Cancel All Open": The grey-outlined button next to the bet buttons deletes every pending mock bet whose game hasn't started yet, across all sports. Bets on games that are already live or final are kept (they're locked-in records by then).

Cancelling a single bet: Open any pending bet's detail page (/mockbets/<id>/) and click the red "Cancel This Bet" button. The button is only shown while the underlying game hasn't started yet — once first pitch / kickoff / tipoff passes, cancellation is no longer possible (the bet is locked in for analytics).

Golf Bets: Navigate to Golf and tap any tournament to open the event detail page. Each golfer in the field is listed with their outright odds and implied probability. Tap "Mock Bet" next to any golfer to open the placement modal pre-filled with that golfer's name and odds. Golf supports 6 bet types: Outright Winner, Top 5/10/20 Finish, Make the Cut, and Head-to-Head Matchup. You can also use the golfer search field in the modal to find any golfer by name.

Settlement: When a game finalizes, pending bets are automatically resolved. Payouts are calculated from the American odds locked in at placement time. Win/Loss/Push results are determined by the actual game outcome. The settlement engine also captures the closing line value (CLV) for each team-sport moneyline bet at this point so analytics can compare your bet price to the market's final close.

My Mock Bets Dashboard: View all your simulated bets with a bankroll summary header (Total Wagered, Total Won, Total Lost, Net Profit, W-L-P record). Each bet card shows stake, payout, net result, the result badge (✓ WIN / ✗ LOSS / PUSH / PENDING), and CLV when available (e.g. +4.2% · Beat Market with the line movement -120 → -135). Filter by sport and result status.

Why This Lost: When a bet settles to a loss, we classify it on the detail page as Bad Luck (good edge, just unlucky), Model Miss (overconfident model), Market Misread (market disagreed with us at close), or Weak Edge (shouldn't have been taken). Bets placed before the snapshot fields landed will show "Unknown" — no honest classification is possible without the at-bet-time data.

Decision Review: After a bet settles, flag it as "Would Repeat" or "Would Avoid" and add reflection notes. This creates a feedback loop for improving your analytical discipline.

Important: Mock Bets is a simulation for analytics and learning. No real money, no payouts, no betting advice. For decision evaluation only.

{# ── 11. Mock Bet Analytics ────────────────────────────────────────── #}

11. Mock Bet Analytics — Postgame Command Center

The Analytics Dashboard is a decision-quality command center that answers five questions: How did I do? · Did I beat the market? · Did the recommendation engine perform? · Were Top Plays better than regular picks? · What should I learn? Access it from the "Analytics Dashboard" button on the My Mock Bets page.

Quick time filters: Chip row at the top — Today / Yesterday / Last 7 days / Last 30 days / All time. Click one and the entire dashboard re-scopes to that window.

System Verdict panel: Color-coded card at the top of the page (green / yellow / red border) announcing STRONG / NEUTRAL / WEAK with a HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW confidence band based on sample size. Bullet list of reasons + warnings spelled out. STRONG requires CLV %+ ≥ 60, ROI > 0, and at least 25 settled bets. WEAK fires on any negative ROI or CLV %+ < 50%. Sample size band is independent of verdict — a STRONG verdict on 8 bets is still LOW confidence.

Hero KPIs: Five large cards — Net P/L, ROI, Record (W-L-P), CLV %+ (% of bets that beat the closing line), System Confidence Score. Each card is color-coded green/red by direction.

AI Postgame Summary: Plain-English narrative answering "Did the system make good decisions?", "Where is the strongest edge?", and "Did we beat the market?" Lazy-loaded panel that calls OpenAI with the deterministic facts only — never invents data, never gives real-money advice. Falls back to a deterministic narrative when OpenAI is unavailable; the panel is never blank. The AI cannot override the System Verdict — it can only summarize and contextualize it.

Result Drivers: Six small lists explaining the day — Best Wins (top 5 by payout), Worst Losses (top 5 by stake), Best CLV, Worst CLV, Biggest Misses (recommended/elite losses), and Best Validations (recommended/elite wins WITH positive CLV — the system did its job).

Market Validation (CLV): Aggregate CLV %+, average CLV, plus the best and worst single bets for the period. CLV is the leading indicator of edge — it resolves at game start, not after hundreds of settled bets.

Where Is My Edge? (Edge Buckets): Performance grouped by snapshot edge bucket: 0-2pp / 2-4pp / 4-6pp / 6pp+. Each row shows count, win rate, ROI, and CLV %+. If a higher bucket consistently outperforms a lower one, the data is telling you to raise the minimum edge threshold.

Recommendation Performance tables: By Status (Recommended vs Not Recommended) and By Tier (Elite / Strong / Standard) — each showing bets, win rate, ROI, net P/L, and CLV %+. Target: Recommended should outperform Not Recommended; Elite should outperform Strong.

Bet Ledger: Full row-by-row table of every bet with When · Sport · Selection · Type · Odds · Stake · Result · Net · CLV · Tier · Status · Decision Quality. The Decision Quality column combines outcome with CLV: Perfect (win + +CLV — the process earned it), Got Lucky (win + -CLV — process didn't), Unlucky (loss + +CLV — variance, process was right), Bad Bet (loss + -CLV — process was wrong), Neutral (push). Bets without CLV captured show "—".

Filters: Narrow your analysis by sport, bet type, confidence level, model source, and date range. All KPIs and charts update to reflect your selection.

Current-rules only filter: A checkbox in the filter bar excludes bets placed before the decision rules + snapshot fields landed. Use this to scope analytics to the post-rules era when comparing to the system's current behavior.

Charts: Five interactive Chart.js visualizations — Cumulative P/L (time-series), Rolling Win % (10-bet sliding window), ROI by Sport (bar chart), Performance by Confidence (grouped bar: win rate + ROI by level), and Odds Distribution (scatter plot of wins vs losses).

House vs User Comparison: A head-to-head table comparing bets made with the House model vs your User model — win rate, ROI, average odds, implied probability, and volatility.

Confidence Calibration: Tests whether your self-rated confidence (Low/Medium/High) aligns with actual outcomes. Shows expected win rate (from implied probability) vs actual win rate.

Edge Analysis: Groups bets by expected edge buckets (negative, 0-3%, 3-7%, 7%+) and shows win rate and ROI for each bucket. Tests whether higher-edge picks actually perform better.

Variance & Stress Testing: Longest win/loss streaks, max drawdown, volatility (standard deviation), and best/worst N-bet stretches. Helps you understand the range of outcomes.

Flat-Bet Simulation: Enter a hypothetical flat stake amount to see what your results would look like if every bet used the same stake. Removes stake-sizing effects from the analysis.

AI Performance Commentary: Tap "Generate Commentary" to get an AI-generated review of your simulated performance. The AI uses your chosen persona (set in Preferences) and analyzes your KPIs, calibration, edge results, and variance data. Requires at least 5 settled bets. The AI model and parameters (temperature, max tokens) are configurable by admins in the Admin Console → Site Configuration.

Important: All figures are simulated. Past simulated performance does not predict future outcomes. For learning and decision evaluation only.

{# ── 12. Glossary ────────────────────────────────────────────────────── #}

12. Glossary

Term Definition
Edge The difference between a model's probability and the market's probability. A +5% edge means the model thinks the team is 5 percentage points more likely to win than the market implies.
House Model The site's fixed probability model (version "v1"). Uses team ratings, home-field advantage, and injury data to compute win probabilities.
User Model Your personalized version of the house model with custom weight multipliers set in My Model.
Market Probability The implied win probability derived from current betting odds. A -150 moneyline implies approximately 60% win probability.
Delta The difference between your user model probability and the house model probability. Shows where you disagree with the house.
Spread The point spread — how many points the favored team is expected to win by. A -7 spread means the favorite is expected to win by 7 points.
Total (Over/Under) The projected combined score of both teams. Typically displayed as a single number (e.g., 145.5).
Moneyline A straight bet on which team wins. Expressed as American odds: -150 means bet $150 to win $100 (favorite), +200 means bet $100 to win $200 (underdog).
Data Confidence Data-input quality (NOT a bet recommendation). Labeled Data: HIGH/MED/LOW in the UI. HIGH = fresh odds + both starters known. MED = odds within 12 hours. LOW = stale odds or missing data. A "Data: HIGH" game can still be "Not Recommended" — they measure different things.
Recommendation Status Recommended or Not Recommended. Determined by decision rules: edge ≥ 4 pp (minimum) AND not a heavy-juice favorite without strong edge. Elite-tier picks (edge ≥ 8 pp) override the juice gate.
Tier Strength of the recommendation. Elite = edge ≥ 8 pp (capped at 2 per slate). Strong = edge ≥ 6 pp. Standard = otherwise.
De-vigged Edge Edge computed against the fair (vig-removed) market probability instead of the raw vig-included implied probability. On a -110/-110 line, the de-vigged baseline is 50/50, not 52.4/52.4.
Decision Quality Per-bet classification combining outcome with CLV. Perfect = win + +CLV. Got Lucky = win + -CLV. Unlucky = loss + +CLV. Bad Bet = loss + -CLV. Neutral = push.
System Verdict Aggregate trust signal in the analytics dashboard. STRONG = CLV %+ ≥ 60 AND ROI > 0 AND ≥ 25 settled bets. WEAK = ROI < 0 OR CLV %+ < 50. NEUTRAL otherwise. Each verdict carries a confidence band (HIGH/MEDIUM/LOW) tied to sample size.
Brier Score A calibration metric for probability predictions. Ranges from 0 (perfect) to 1 (worst). A coin flip scores 0.250. Lower is better.
CLV (Closing Line Value) Whether your bet's price beat the market's final close. Positive CLV = you got a better price than what the line settled at by game start. Computed as bet_decimal_odds − close_decimal_odds at settlement. Captured for moneyline bets on team sports when a pre-game OddsSnapshot exists. Surfaced as +4.2% · Beat Market on the bet row plus the line movement (-120 → -135).
Calibration How closely a model's predicted probabilities match actual outcomes. If the model says 70% for 100 games, about 70 should actually win.
Snapshot A frozen capture of the model's prediction and market odds for a game, taken within 24 hours of game time. Used for performance tracking.
HFA (Home Field Advantage) The statistical edge a team gets from playing at home. The model applies a fixed bonus (3.0 for CFB, 3.5 for CBB) multiplied by the HFA weight.
