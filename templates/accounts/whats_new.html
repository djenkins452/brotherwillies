Apr 27, 2026 — Tiered Intelligence Phase 3 (Promoted Recommendations)

Phase 3 graduates statistically proven Spread/Total Leans into true Recommendations — the green-tier trust level previously reserved for Moneyline. With break-even display so you can see profit clarity at a glance, and source-safety guards that mean ESPN-source rows can never reach the green tier regardless of stats.

The trust-tier ladder, finalized

Tier Marker Bet Type Bulk-Bet
Recommendation (Moneyline) 🟢 Green pill Moneyline Active
Recommendation (Proven Edge) (NEW) 🟢 Green badge w/ stats Spread, Total Active when promoted
Lean 🟡 Yellow badge w/ stats Spread, Total Disabled
Signal Only Cyan info pill Spread, Total Disabled

When does a Lean become a Recommendation?

  • Spread: sample ≥ 50 AND win rate ≥ 54.5% AND ROI ≥ 2.0%
  • Total: sample ≥ 50 AND win rate ≥ 55.0% AND ROI ≥ 2.5% (stricter — total markets are noisier)
  • Source-safety: ESPN-source rows and synthesized (derived) rows are never promoted, regardless of stats. Recommendations require both "the signal works" AND "we trust THIS row's price."

What you'll see (when enabled)

  • Two new green-bordered sections: 🟢 Spread Recommendations (Proven Edge) and 🟢 Total Recommendations (Proven Edge). Same trust family as the moneyline Recommended Plays group.
  • Upgraded Lean badge format with break-even comparison: 🟡 Lean (56.0% vs 52.4% break-even, 42 games) — you see the gap to profitability inline.
  • New green Recommended badge: 🟢 Recommended (57.2% vs 52.4%, +3.1% ROI, 120 games)
  • New 🟢 Recommended filter chip alongside the existing Leans Only chip.
  • Active 🟢 Bet All Spread (N) and 🟢 Bet All Total (N) bulk buttons — replaces the Phase 1 "Coming Soon" disabled buttons when promoted recs exist for that bet type.
  • "Bet All Verified Plays" renamed to "Bet All Moneyline Plays" so the bet type is unambiguous alongside the new spread/total bulk buttons.
  • New "Promoted Recommendations (Proven Edge)" panel on /profile/performance/ — shows per-signal-type win rate, ROI, sample for any signal type currently above the promotion bar.

Hard guarantees

  • Moneyline pipeline remains frozen. Three sentinel tests now lock down the recommendation engine's ignorance of Phase 1/2/3 fields.
  • Promoted rows render exactly once — in their green Proven Edge section, not also in the cyan Opportunity Signals section.
  • ESPN-source promotion is blocked at both the data layer AND the bulk-bet placement layer (defense in depth).

Phase 3 will only show promoted recommendations once enough settled history accumulates to clear the 50-game minimum at the stricter win-rate + ROI bars — give it a few weeks of live data after Phase 1+2 are flipped.

Apr 27, 2026 — Tiered Intelligence Phase 2 (Lean Layer)

Phase 2 promotes spread and total opportunity signals from passive markers to evidence-based "Lean" markers when historical data supports them. Lean ≠ Recommended — the yellow badge is intentionally distinct from the green Recommended tier so users can never confuse them. Bulk betting on Lean opportunities is still disabled.

Three trust tiers, never blurred

Tier Marker Bet Type
Recommendation 🟢 Green Recommended pill Moneyline
Lean (NEW) 🟡 Yellow Lean badge with stats Spread, Total (when historical performance supports it)
Signal Only Cyan info pill Spread, Total (no Lean status yet)

When does a signal become a Lean?

  • Spread: historical win rate > 53% over a 30+ game sample.
  • Total: historical win rate > 54% AND ROI > 2% over a 30+ game sample (stricter — total markets are noisier).
  • Lean status is snapshotted at signal-creation time. Once stamped, it never changes — threshold tweaks or data drift can't retroactively re-label an old row.
  • Pushes are excluded from win-rate math (standard sports-betting convention) but counted in raw totals for transparency.

What you'll see (when enabled)

  • Yellow Lean badge inline on each opportunity card when historical data supports the signal: 🟡 Lean (56.0% win rate, 42 games).
  • "(Market Signals Only)" subtitle on the Spread / Total section headers when the leans layer is active.
  • "🟡 Leans Only" filter chip alongside All / Moneyline / Spread / Total. Click it to isolate just the data-supported leans.
  • New "Spread & Total Performance" panel at /profile/performance/ — per-signal-type win rate, ROI estimate, sample size, push count, and Lean? marker.

Hard guarantees

  • Moneyline recommendations remain the ONLY "Recommended" tier. Lean ≠ Recommended.
  • Bulk betting on Spread / Total stays disabled in Phase 2 — the Lean layer surfaces evidence, not edge.
  • The recommendation engine has zero awareness of Phase 2 fields or performance functions (test-enforced).

Phase 3 (future): true modeling for spread + totals. Once a stable lean shows multi-month positive ROI, we can graduate it to "Recommended" by wiring its own edge calculation and activating its bulk button.

Apr 27, 2026 — Source-Aware Betting + MLB Hub UX

A wave of trust-and-clarity work. You can now see at a glance where every displayed odd came from, the MLB hub groups recommended vs not-recommended into clearly-bordered sections, bulk-bet buttons tell you exactly how many bets you’re about to place, and a sweep of MLB odds-ingestion fixes restored 100% match rate against the paid Odds API.

Source-Aware Betting — trust badges everywhere

  • 🟢 Verified Odds — the line came from the paid Odds API. Authoritative; recommendations behave as before.
  • 🟡 ESPN Odds — the line came from ESPN’s free fallback feed (used when primary is missing or stale). Recommendations still issue, but the displayed confidence is reduced (×0.85) to reflect lower trust in the source.
  • 🔴 Derived Odds — the moneyline was synthesized via symmetric inversion when only one side was published. Never feeds a recommendation, never appears in bulk-bet buttons, only visible in staff diagnostics.
  • Badges appear on every game tile on the MLB hub, on the MLB game-detail page (next to the existing Data: HIGH pill), and the recommendation engine respects them automatically.

MLB hub — decision group boxes

  • 👍 Recommended Plays (green-bordered box) wraps Top Plays Today, Recommended Bets (verified), and Recommended Bets (ESPN Fallback) under one heading: “Model says: BET — edge cleared the threshold.”
  • ⚠️ Not Recommended (gray-bordered box) wraps today’s fades. Always renders — if there’s nothing to fade today you see a positive empty-state, never silent absence.
  • Inner section headers stay (elite vs standard, verified vs ESPN, today’s fades) so you don’t lose the existing breakdown — the outer group just frames the parent classification.

Bulk-bet buttons now show the count

  • Buttons read 🟢 Bet All Verified Plays (8) and 🟡 Bet All ESPN Plays (3) — you see the count before you click.
  • Confirm modal: “You are about to place a $100 mock bet on each of 8 recommended games (verified primary-source odds). Games where you already have a pending bet will be skipped automatically. Proceed?”
  • Today-slate scope fix: previously the button could place bets on tomorrow’s games too (which carried their own recommendations under the “Upcoming” list). Now the count and the placement match exactly what’s visible on screen.

MLB game-detail — trust-aware odds picker

  • The game-detail page used to show whichever snapshot was most recent, regardless of source. Now it prefers fresh primary (paid API) over fresh secondary (ESPN), only falling back to the most recent of any source if nothing fresh exists.
  • A 30-second-old ESPN row will no longer shadow a 10-minute-old paid-API row. The displayed spread, total, moneyline, and edge math all flow from the most-trusted available snapshot.

MLB odds reliability — the silent fixes

  • Doubleheader matching — the persist used to match by “earliest first pitch with these teams,” which broke whenever a doubleheader was scheduled. It now matches by closest commence time, so each game in a doubleheader gets its own snapshots.
  • ESPN persist freshness check — replaced a brittle target-game-IDs filter that was rejecting valid ESPN matches. ESPN persist now self-filters per row by checking whether the specific game already has a fresh primary snapshot.
  • Comprehensive 30-team alias coverage + fuzzy match — closes a class of “API gave us a name we couldn’t map to a DB team” bugs.
  • Confirmed via the new in-product persist summary: 100% of API events now match a DB game (10 of 10 on the latest run, 73 snapshots written).

There’s also a wave of operations-side improvements (bigger Recent Runs transcripts, copy-to-clipboard, captured subcommand stdout, MLB gap diagnostic) that aren’t user-facing but make every future investigation faster.

Apr 26, 2026 — Odds Intelligence (UI + Analytics)

The market movement system from yesterday now shows up on tiles, the game detail page, and a new performance panel. The recommendation engine gets a small bounded nudge when sharp money agrees, and a warning chip when sharp money disagrees — but never a downgrade.

What you'll see

  • MLB hub tiles — new chip alongside the recommendation badges:
    • 📈 Market Support (green) — sharp money is moving toward the recommended pick
    • 📉 Market Against You (red) — sharp money is moving against the recommended pick; proceed with caution
    • ↗ Market Moving (blue) — meaningful movement detected, direction doesn't strongly favor either side
  • MLB game detail — a "Market Movement" card explaining the signal and how it affected the displayed confidence.
  • Profile → Performance — new "Market Movement Agreement" table comparing your win rate, ROI, and CLV across three buckets: market agreed / market disagreed / no signal.

Decision integration is conservative

  • Sharp + with the model → displayed confidence nudges up by +5pp (capped, clamped at 99% — we never claim certainty).
  • Strong + with the model → +3pp. Moderate + with → +1pp.
  • Sharp/strong + against the model → warning chip only. Confidence and tier are unchanged. The model stays the source of truth; the warning is just transparency about a signal you might want to factor in.

Provider rollout: hook is live for MLB, CFB, CBB, and college baseball. Golf has a different schema (per-golfer outright odds, not a two-sided market) and is intentionally deferred.

{# ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ #} {# Apr 25 — Odds Intelligence (Foundation) #} {# ═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════ #}

Apr 25, 2026 — Odds Intelligence (Foundation)

A new system to capture, score, and classify market movement. Phase 1 — invisible to users but the math is now wired in. The visible UI follows on Apr 26.

What it does

  • Every Odds API pull writes a snapshot row tagged raw. When a follow-up snapshot crosses a significance threshold (≥7 cents on the line OR ≥2pp de-vigged probability shift OR ≥0.5 pts spread/total), the row is upgraded to significant and scored 0–100.
  • Score components weighted: 40% magnitude / 25% speed / 20% direction consistency / 15% timing (recent moves count more).
  • Classification cuts: 0–25 noise / 25–55 moderate / 55–80 strong / 80+ sharp.
  • Storage policy: raw snapshots pruned after 14 days. Significant / closing / bet-context snapshots kept forever.

Live for MLB end-to-end on this date. Schema additions for all sports so the next phase ports cleanly.

Apr 25, 2026 — Ops Command Center

A live, single-page picture of system health for superusers. Built so we never again have to read deploy logs to figure out "is the data stale because the API is down, the cron crashed, or quota is exhausted?"

What it shows

  • Overall banner — green / yellow / red / unknown, computed from the worst component.
  • Odds API health dial — call count, failure count, average latency over the last 24h. Goes red on any failure in the last hour.
  • Quota dial — % of monthly Odds API allowance used (read from response headers), with a colored meter.
  • Cron pipeline dial — last status of refresh_data and refresh_scores_and_settle, plus 7-day success/failure counts. Detects rows stuck in running for >10 min as crashed workers.
  • Recent failures — combined timeline of API + cron failures with status codes and timestamps.
  • Recent runs — table of the last 15 cron runs with trigger, duration, and summary.

Manual controls

  • Run Full Data Refresh — spawns the heavy refresh in a background subprocess. Anti-overlap guard blocks duplicate concurrent runs.
  • Run Score Refresh + Settle — lightweight 15-min pipeline (no quota cost).
  • Test Odds API — synchronous probe of /v4/sports. On 401 the dashboard tells you exactly what to do (rotate key in Railway → Variables).

Access: superusers only, at /ops/command-center/ (also linked from the Profile page).

Apr 25, 2026 — Decision Intelligence Era

A series of upgrades that take the system from "show interesting analysis" to "tell me what to bet, why, and whether the system is actually working." All changes preserve every game on the page — nothing is hidden — and the math is honest about its own limits.

🎯 Recommendation engine + tiers

Every game with odds now produces a single concrete pick: team + bet type + line + odds. Picks are classified into Elite (edge ≥ 8 pp), Strong (≥ 6 pp), or Standard, with a slate-level cap of 2 elites max so the "🔥 High Confidence" signal stays rare. A separate decision-rule layer marks each pick Recommended or Not Recommended based on edge thresholds and a heavy-favorite juice gate.

🛡 De-vigged edges (more honest math)

Edge is now computed against the fair (vig-removed) market probability instead of the raw vig-included implied probability. On a -110/-110 line that means the baseline is 50/50, not 52.4/52.4. Some bets that previously looked like value drop out; some that looked like fades become real opportunities. Fewer recommendations, but the ones we make are real.

📈 CLV (Closing Line Value) tracking

Every settled bet now records the closing market price and computes CLV — did your bet's price beat the close? Positive CLV is the professional's leading-indicator signal of real edge: it resolves at game start (not after 500 settled bets) and survives losses. Surfaced as +4.2% · Beat Market on the bet row, with the full line movement (-120 → -135).

🏟 MLB Hub → Decision Board

The MLB hub used to be a schedule-grouped page (Live / Today / Upcoming). It's now organized around what the system thinks: 🔥 Top Plays Today (elite tier, capped at 2), 👍 Recommended Bets, and ⚠ Not Recommended (dimmed, still clickable). Every game still appears — the partition is by decision quality, not by hiding. CTAs adapt: "Bet This" (green) for recommended, "Not Recommended" (grey but clickable) for fades.

📊 Postgame Command Center

Mock Bet Analytics got a top-to-bottom redesign:

  • System Verdict panel — STRONG / NEUTRAL / WEAK with HIGH/MED/LOW confidence band based on sample size. Reasons + warnings spelled out so you know exactly why.
  • Hero KPIs — Net P/L · ROI · Record · CLV %+ · System Confidence in five large cards above everything else.
  • Result Drivers — Best Wins, Worst Losses, Best/Worst CLV, Biggest Misses (recommended/elite losses), Best Validations (recommended wins with positive CLV).
  • Where Is My Edge? — Performance by edge bucket (0-2pp / 2-4pp / 4-6pp / 6pp+) with win rate, ROI, and CLV %+ per bucket.
  • Decision Quality column — every settled bet pilled as Perfect (win+CLV) / Got Lucky / Unlucky / Bad Bet / Neutral.
  • Quick time filters — Today / Yesterday / 7d / 30d / All time chips at the top.
  • AI Postgame Summary — plain-English narrative answering "did the system make good decisions?", "where is the strongest edge?", "did we beat the market?" Falls back to a deterministic narrative when OpenAI is unavailable; never blank.
  • Full Bet Ledger — 12-column table with every bet and its CLV, tier, status, and Decision Quality.

🔍 Why This Lost

When a bet settles to a loss, we now classify it as variance (good edge, just unlucky), model_error (high confidence, no edge), market_movement (market disagreed with us at close), or bad_edge (shouldn't have taken it). Surfaced on the bet detail page with a "Bad Luck / Model Miss / Weak Edge / Market Misread" badge plus the actual miss numbers.

⚡ Bulk actions on the MLB hub

Two new buttons in the hub header. "Bet All Recommended" places a $100 mock bet on every game whose recommendation is "recommended" and you don't already have a pending bet on — transactional, idempotent, runs the same eligibility guards as per-bet placement. "Cancel All Open" deletes every pending mock bet whose game hasn't started yet, across all sports. Both gated by a confirm prompt.

↩ Cancel a single bet

Bet detail pages now show a red "Cancel This Bet" button when the underlying game hasn't started yet. Click → confirm → the bet is deleted from your record. Once the game's first pitch passes (status flips OR the time check fires), the button disappears — bets locked in stay locked in.

🎯 "Why This Is a Top Play" on elite cards

Elite-tier model picks now include a bulleted explanation directly on the banner: edge in pp, market mispricing call when edge ≥ 8 pp, top-of-slate framing, projected win probability when ≥ 70%. Pure presentation — no new modeling, just surfacing the reasoning the system already used.

🔧 Operations & reliability

Lightweight 15-minute "scores + settle" cron path (separate from the 6-hour heavy data refresh). Configurable score-update window via env vars. Per-provider health logs and structured cycle summaries. Robust ESPN odds fallback with multi-shape moneyline extraction. Explicit commenceTimeFrom window on the primary Odds API call so today's games stay in the payload even close to first pitch.

For complete commit history, see the docs/changelog.md file in the repo. As always, simulated analytics — not advice. Past simulated performance does not predict future outcomes.

Apr 19, 2026 — MLB Injury Ingestion

Superstar on the IL? We should know. MLB now ingests injury data per team from ESPN and surfaces the most-severe player per side on every tile — plus boosts the signal layer accordingly.

⚕ On every tile

Live and upcoming MLB tiles now show the most-severe current injury per team, e.g. ATL ⚕ SP Spencer Strider (15-Day IL, return 2026-05-01). Red-tinted, compact, scannable. Only rendered when a team actually has a meaningful injury.

Smart severity mapping

Starting pitchers get boosted — a 10-Day IL for an SP is high impact because losing a starter changes the game-level forecast. Position players on short ILs get lower weights. 15-Day and 60-Day ILs are always high. Unknown statuses (Probable, etc.) are ignored rather than mis-classified.

Feeds the confidence engine

Injury impact already fed our priority scoring; it now also propagates into the confidence number and can trigger the Key injury reason on tiles. High-impact injuries can demote a Best Bet candidate.

Apr 19, 2026 — Confidence, Focus Engine & ESPN Odds Fallback

Four layered improvements that take the MLB hub from "here are options" to "here's the single best spot, how strong it is, and what you've already acted on."

Confidence scoring (0–100%)

Every game now carries a deterministic confidence number blended from line-value discrepancy (40% weight), live-game signals, and data completeness (odds present, starters known). Primary action chips visualize it with a thin bottom bar; the Focus banner shows the exact percentage. Blowouts clamp the ceiling to 40% — the game's already decided.

🔥 Focus Right Now banner

A single card at the top of the MLB hub surfaces the one game most worth your attention: highest-confidence primary action, with reason and a confidence mini-bar. If nothing meets the bar, the banner is simply omitted — we never fake a focus.

🎯 Bet Placed (user state aware)

When you already have a pending mock bet on a game, its primary action becomes Bet Placed and the Mock Bet button swaps to a View Bet link. The original Best Bet / Watch Now recommendations stay visible as secondary context — we never hide information.

ESPN DraftKings odds fallback

When The Odds API returns zero rows (quota exhausted, unavailable bookmakers, or just no coverage), we now fall back to ESPN's public scoreboard JSON which carries DraftKings lines. No API key, no quota overlap. Combined with widened ±36h team/game matching and a nearest-by-delta Python-side fallback, odds coverage is dramatically more reliable.

Apr 19, 2026 — Guided Choice & Odds Integrity

Evolve from "here are the games" to "here's the single best spot on the board — and why — backed by real data."

Primary vs Secondary actions

Action tags now carry a strength. When both 💰 Best Bet and 🔥 Watch Now fire on the same game, Best Bet is the Primary action (filled pill) and Watch Now becomes Secondary (outlined). Exactly one Primary per game. Nothing is hidden — the hierarchy just makes the best spot obvious.

★ Top Opportunity (scarcity by design)

Across every live and today's tile, only the single highest-confidence Best Bet gets a gold ★ Top Opportunity tag. Scarcity is the point. One tag you can trust beats ten you have to second-guess. Configurable via settings.MLB_MAX_TOP_OPPORTUNITIES if you want more.

Line-value signal (real edge)

New signal: line_value_discrepancy = |house_prob − market_prob|. When the gap is ≥ 6%, the game gets a Model edge vs. market reason and Best Bet prefers this over a tight-spread trigger. This is a real "we see something the market doesn't" signal, not just a thin-line bet.

Structured reasons (no more UI strings in the service layer)

Reasons are now structured keys (tight_spread, ace_matchup, line_value, late_game…) and the UI maps them to labels at render time. Same key can render differently in the tile vs. help modal vs. analytics without any service-layer change.

Fail-fast odds pipeline

We never silently operate with zero odds any more. The API client rejects HTML responses served as 200s (rate-limit pages, WAF challenges) with an explicit error that includes URL, status, and a body snippet. ingest_odds raises in DEBUG when zero snapshots are created and logs a high-severity error in prod. A post-ingest sanity check catches the case where rows wrote but no games-of-today got coverage.

Pitcher-weighted prediction model

The baseball house model weights the starting-pitcher matchup at 65%, team rating at 35%, plus a modest home-field adjustment (2.5 for MLB, 2.0 for college baseball). Pitcher ratings are derived from season ERA, WHIP, and K/9 on a 10–95 scale. If either starter is unknown, pitcher contribution is zero and confidence drops to low — no fabrication.

Mock bets on MLB and college baseball

Under the hood

Added a sport_registry that's now the single source of truth for team-sport metadata — the brittle "kickoff-or-tipoff" ternary is gone, and adding a sixth team sport is a single registry entry instead of a file sweep. Settlement logic for CFB/CBB/MLB/CB now dispatches through one shared helper instead of per-sport copies.

{# Mock Bets - Core #}

Mock Bet Simulation

A brand new system for tracking simulated betting decisions without real money. Place mock bets from any game detail page, track outcomes over time, and analyze your decision quality.

  • Place bets — Moneyline, spread, and total bets for CFB and CBB. Golf supports outright winner, top 5/10/20 finish, make the cut, and head-to-head matchups.
